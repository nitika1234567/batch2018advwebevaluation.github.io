      History Of The Great Wall Of China

      • The history of the Great Wall of China dates back to the eras of Spring and Autumn (771 to 476 BC) and Warring States (475 to 221 BC) when several states constructed fortifications for the purpose of their defense.
      • When Qin Shi Huang became the emperor of Unified China, he established the Qin Dynasty (221 to 206 BC) and linked all the walls to protect his newly founded empire from the invasion of Eurasian nomads.
      • Qin’s wall was extended from the northwestern Chinese region Gansu to the coastline of southern Manchuria.
      • The Han Dynasty (202 BC to 220 AD), which was the next imperial Chinese dynasty after Qin, made the wall longer that stretched from the Yang Pass in the south to the Yumen Pass to the north.
      • After the Han Dynasty, the dynasties of the Northern Qi (550 to 574 AD) and the Sui (589 to 618 AD) rebuilt and extended the walls, however, they also changed their routes.
      • The Ming Dynasty (1368 to 1644 AD) then rebuilt 5,500 miles (8,850 km) long wall that began in the west from Jiayu Pass and ended in Shanhai Pass to the sea.
      • The Ming Wall then continuously went and ended at the Hushan Great Wall in Manchuria region.
